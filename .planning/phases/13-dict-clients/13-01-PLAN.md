**Key facts already verified (do not re-derive):**
- `WindowsAuthenticationMiddleware.InvokeAsync` already adds `new Claim("Permission", "EditDictionary")`
  when `dbUser.CanEditDictionary` is true (from `emp.access_edit_dictionary`). No DB or middleware
  change is needed — the claim exists; only a POLICY that consumes it is missing.
- Existing policies live in `Program.cs` inside `builder.Services.AddAuthorization(...)`: `DatabaseUser`
  (fallback) and `RandomDrugScreen`.
- Existing requirements/handlers are in `Authorization/DatabaseUserAuthorizationHandler.cs`
  (`DatabaseUserRequirement`, `RandomDrugScreenRequirement`, and their handlers). The RDS handler
  succeeds when `DbUserValidated == "true"` AND (`IsAdministrator == "True"` OR
  `CanAccessRandomDrugScreen == "True"`). Mirror this pattern.
- `NavMenu.razor` uses `<AuthorizeView Policy="RandomDrugScreen">` to gate the RDS section and plain
  `<div class="nav-item px-3">` + `<NavLink class="nav-link" href="...">` with Open Iconic (`oi oi-*`)
  icons for links.
</context>
