signed BAA + encryption-at-rest + enforced TLS-in-transit + native audit logging (`pgaudit`) +
KMS key management — and that Npgsql connects to it with TLS required. Then consolidate all four
Phase 21 spikes into a single `FINDINGS.md` that sizes Phases 22-25.

Purpose: §4 shows broad BAA availability, but SKU/feature-level eligibility varies and gates the
security review. This plan also closes out Phase 21 by rolling the four findings files into one
sizing document the downstream phases consume.
Output: `.planning/phases/21-derisking-spikes/21-findings-hosting.md` and the consolidated
`.planning/phases/21-derisking-spikes/FINDINGS.md`.

Contains a human-action checkpoint: standing up a managed instance and executing a BAA requires
cloud-account/billing and legal access that is not automatable here.
</objective>
